Shane Gillis is my favorite comedian because of how funny he is.

While that is an obvious reason to like a comedian, I think the risks he takes balancing being offensive and being funny, how he uses his position to help his friends find success, and how he employs (often uncomfortable) aspects of U.S. History in his jokes are what makes him stand out among other comedians of his age and cohort.

For those who aren't aware, Shane was a cast member on Saturday Night Live for a week. He was fired before the season started when it was discovered that he had used racist slurs and made homophobic jokes on a podcast years prior to being cast on SNL.

Since being fired from SNL, he has been a guest on many podcasts, has a podcast of his own, has a show on Netflix called Tires and, after apologizing for his insensitive jokes, was even invited back to Saturday Night Live to host an episode.

Personally, I like the image of him at the Mall of America because I have performed stand-up comedy at that club and bombed so hard with my own poorly thought out, badly timed, insensitive jokes that I was never able to find the courage to get back on stage after the MC chastised me and the crowd turned on me. While Shane has definitely said things in his past that are offensive and regrettable, I think he is a good example of how someone can learn from their mistakes, grow as a person, and work to comeback from life's setbacks--for him that meant getting back up on stage, contiuing to tell risky jokes (with a new found attention to content), make people laugh, and do what he loves.
